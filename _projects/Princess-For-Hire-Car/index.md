## Brief
While listening to BBC Radio 1, I heard an interview with an entrepreneur who had started a "Princess for Hire" business, bringing performers dressed as popular fairy-tale characters to children's birthday parties.
The conversation sparked an idea: if the character appearance creates excitement, how could the arrival experience be made even more memorable?
Rather than arriving in a standard vehicle, I explored the idea of a themed car wrapped in colourful vinyl graphics that would create a sense of anticipation before the performer even stepped out of the vehicle. The concept aimed to strengthen the business's brand identity while providing an additional touchpoint for creating memorable experiences for children and families.
## Design Approach
Using a combination of generative AI imagery and graphic design techniques, I developed a vehicle wrap concept inspired by storybooks, castles, and fantasy themes. The artwork was designed to be eye-catching from a distance, functioning both as a marketing tool and as part of the overall entertainment experience.
The project demonstrates how thoughtful design can help small businesses differentiate themselves from competitors by turning everyday assets into opportunities for branding and customer engagement.
## Outcome
Although this was a personal concept project, it highlights my interest in identifying opportunities to improve products and services through design. By considering the complete customer journey, even something as simple as a vehicle can contribute to a stronger brand presence and a more memorable experience.
